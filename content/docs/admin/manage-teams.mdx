---
title: "Manage Teams"
description: "Create, update, archive, reactivate, and delete Teams."
---

Company Owners and Company Admins manage Teams from **Team > Teams** in Command
Centre.

## Create a Team

1. Open **All Teams**.
2. Select **Create Team**.
3. Enter the Team name.
4. Review the generated slug.
5. Select an existing active user as the initial Team Owner.
6. Select **Create Team**.

The slug is the permanent workspace identifier. You can change the Team name
later, but you cannot change its slug.

Every Team starts with an active Team Owner. A Company Admin who creates a Team
also receives a Company Full access grant for that Team.

## Manage people

Open a Team and select **Members**.

* Use **Add an existing user** when the person already has an active Team OS
  account. Choose Member or Admin. The person does not need another invite or
  password.
* Use the selected Team's main **Members** section to invite a new user by
  email. The invite link is valid for 7 days and can be used once.

Team Owners are managed from **Settings**. Add another active owner before
changing an owner's role. Every active Team must keep at least one active
owner.

See [Members](/docs/admin/members) for the full invite flow.

## Update or archive a Team

Open a Team and select **Settings**.

* **Save name** changes the display name. It does not change the slug.
* **Archive** blocks normal Team access and Team authority changes. It also
  cancels pending company access requests for that Team.
* **Reactivate** restores the Team so authorized users can manage and use it
  again.

Company Owners and Company Admins need Full access to update, archive, or
reactivate an existing Team.

## Delete a Team permanently

Only the Company Owner can delete a Team permanently.

1. Archive the Team.
2. Wait at least 30 days.
3. Open **Settings**.
4. Select **Delete permanently**.
5. Enter the exact Team name.
6. Confirm the deletion again.

Permanent deletion cannot be undone. Normal backup retention still applies.
Team data and earlier Team audit events are removed. One minimal
`team.deleted` Company audit event remains.

## Transfer Company Ownership

The current Company Owner can transfer ownership to an active Company Admin.

1. Open **Team > Teams > Company Admins**.
2. Open the target admin's action menu.
3. Select **Transfer ownership**.
4. Confirm the transfer.

The target becomes the Company Owner. The previous owner becomes a Company
Admin. Direct Team roles do not change.

## Recover a missing Company Owner

Use recovery only when no active Company Owner exists. Run it in the Team OS
maintenance environment:

```powershell
npm run company:owner-recover -- --user <EMAIL_OR_USER_ID>
```

The target must be an active user with a working Team OS account. The command
refuses to create a second active Company Owner.
